Short bio

Pacôme Trousselle is a researcher in urban economic geography, specializing in the analysis of economic flows and territorial transformations associated with digital platforms. Trained in geography and spatial planning at Sorbonne University in Paris, where he completed the Master GAED GéoSuds program, he was a Research Fellow at the Institute of Technology and Society of Rio. There, he examined the dynamics of value circulation and its spatial distribution within the city.

His methodological approach combines spatial analysis using geographic information systems with the structuring of multisource datasets within an interdisciplinary research environment, in support of his doctoral work. He seeks to leverage aggregated money flow data and large scale data infrastructures to develop auditable territorial indicators that contribute to scientific knowledge production and serve as strategic tools for public authorities, financial institutions, and international organizations.
